FIRST GUNS

Fire lances-In the 10th century, Chinese inventors created fire lances—tubes filled with gunpowder and pellet projectiles mounted on spears. These portable fire lances were operable by a single person and were used effectively as shock weapons during the Siege of De’an in 11321. The fire lance barrels eventually evolved into metal tubes, transforming into metal-barreled hand cannons.

Hand Cannons- By the 13th century, fire lance barrels were replaced with metal tubes, giving rise to hand cannons. These early firearms spread throughout Eurasia during the 14th century. They were loaded with high-nitrate gunpowder and projectiles, and their basic features included a metal barrel and a properly sized projectile

Oldest Surviving Firearm -The Heilongjiang hand cannon, dating back to 1288, is the oldest surviving firearm. It was discovered in modern-day Acheng District, where battles were fought. Jurchen military commander Li Ting led foot soldiers armed with hand cannons to suppress the rebellion of the Christian Mongol Prince Nayan

Maxim Gun -he world’s first machine gun was the Maxim gun, developed by British inventor Sir Hiram Maxim in 1884

Self-Loading Rifle - The Mondragón rifle, designed in 1908 by Mexican general Manuel Mondragón, became the world’s first successful self-loading rifle
